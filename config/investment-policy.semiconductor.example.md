# Investment Policy Statement (IPS) — EXAMPLE: Semiconductors

This is a *filled-in example* to show what a completed IPS looks like. To use it, copy
it to `config/investment-policy.md` and adjust to your own situation. Values here are
illustrative, not recommendations.

---

## 1. Universe (what to consider)

- **Sectors / themes of interest:** semiconductors, AI infrastructure, semiconductor
  equipment, advanced packaging
- **Explicit watchlist tickers:** NVDA, AMD, TSM, AVGO, ASML, AMAT, MU, ARM (leave any
  blank to let the research agent surface names within the sector)
- **Hard exclusions:** none

## 2. Goals & horizon

- **Objective:** long-term capital growth, high-conviction holdings.
- **Holding horizon:** long-term; do not trade in and out on noise.
- **Minimum conviction to act (1–10):** 7
- **Rebalance cadence:** review daily, act at most weekly unless a real catalyst.

## 3. Position & concentration caps (HARD)

- **Max single position:** 8% of agentic account value
- **Max sector exposure:** 25% of agentic account value
  *(note: a semis-only watchlist makes this cap especially important — don't let the
  whole account become one correlated bet)*
- **Max total positions:** 15
- **Max NEW positions per day:** 2
- **Per-trade max notional (Phase 2 small size):** $200
- **Minimum cash buffer (never deploy below this):** 10% of account

## 4. Risk limits & circuit breakers (HARD)

- **Daily loss breaker:** if the account is down 3% intraday → propose NO new buys.
- **Drawdown breaker:** if the account is down 15% from its peak → HALT all buying.
- **Per-position stop-loss guidance:** flag any holding down 20% from cost for review.
- **No margin, no options, no shorting, no leverage, no crypto.** Ever.

## 5. Benchmark

- **Benchmark:** S&P 500 (SPY). *(Consider also eyeing SOXX/SMH as a sector benchmark,
  since beating the S&P with a semis bet during a chip rally isn't real skill.)*
- **Evaluation windows:** rolling 3, 6, and 12 months, risk-adjusted (Sharpe).

## 6. Execution (Phase 2)

- Every order requires explicit human approval in-session before placement.
- Start small. Scale size only after a documented track record.
- Confirm Robinhood's own trade preview matches the approved order before placing.

---

### Notes to self
- Semis are highly cyclical and headline-driven (export controls, hyperscaler capex).
  Lean on the `devils-advocate` agent here — it's easy to buy a chip name at a top.
